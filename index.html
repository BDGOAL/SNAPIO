Critical Analysis

Support volume reached critical mass between Dec 20-24, coinciding with the peak rental season. Technical malfunctions currently account for 61.8% of all new conversation threads, significantly diluting the focus on broadcast lead conversion.

Operational Risk

High malfunction frequency is a leading indicator of hardware age or maintenance negligence.

B2B Lead Performance

• Conversion Efficiency: 28/42 inquiries were successfully identified as qualified CRM contacts.

• Broadcast Re-engagement: While outreach hit 3,105 accounts, inquiry volume remains static at 1.5/day.

• Action: Revise broadcast CTA to focus on urgency ("Limited Q1 availability") to boost the inquiry-to-lead ratio.

Technical Support Breakdown

• SLA Impact: The 18-minute average response time failed to meet the < 5-minute standard in 78% of reported cases during spikes.

• Spike Analysis: Dec 20 alone generated 20 unique malfunction tickets, representing a systemic localized failure.

• Messaging Density: Over 139 messages were required to resolve cases on Dec 13, highlighting complex troubleshooting needs.

Final Report Summary

The reporting period concludes with a clear dichotomy between SNAPIO's marketing success and its operational stability. Our broadcast strategy has successfully established a wide communication net, yet the resulting B2B inquiry volume is currently suppressed by a heavy reactive workload.

The most pressing concern is the SLA Violation regarding device malfunctions. With support cases (68) now outnumbering sales inquiries (42), the business is in a "Maintenance-First" state rather than a "Growth-First" state.

Key Strategic Directives:
  • Conduct a 48-hour hardware audit on all high-use rental units.
  • Implement automated "Lead Qualification" to free up human support staff.
  • Optimize response protocols to return to the 5-minute SLA standard.
